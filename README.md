# liminal

**the game is what you say.**

---

## is this a game?

A game doesn't require points, enemies, levels, or winning.
What it requires is a loop, agency, and meaning that accumulates.
Liminal has all three.

**the loop:** open → write → save or release → return.
That's a game loop. Identical in structure to checking in on a
Tamagotchi, tending a farm in Stardew, or pulling a card in a
daily card game. The loop is the game.

**agency:** every decision in Liminal is a player choice.
What you write. Whether you save or release. Whether you export
or let it disappear. Whether you share one sentence with your
witness or keep it. The destiny model is a branching narrative
where you author every branch. That's more agency than most
games give you.

**meaning that accumulates:** your entries over time are the score.
Not a number — a body of work. A record of days you showed up.
The corpus growing is the progression system.
Day 60 you wrote a paragraph. That's the high score.

Some people will push back. *"That's not a game, it's an app."*
That's fine. You don't need to win that argument.
If that's what works for you, you're right.
Open it anyway.

---

## what it's for

One good thing. Every day. Private. Yours.

Not a wellness app. Not a therapy tool. Not a habit tracker.
A place for things that are private, good, or precious.
A loop you return to.

It need not be tender. But it may well be.

**it might hold:**
- a celebration nobody else saw yet
- bullet journal entries, quick and honest
- notes to remember — something said, something noticed
- something you're not ready to name
- a quiet win from a hard day
- a moment too good to lose, too personal to post
- something you want to put down somewhere safe
  before deciding what to do with it

---

## how it works

Open it. There's almost nothing there.
A date. A cursor. One prompt.

Write one thing.
Save it, release it, export it, or share one sentence with someone you trust.
Close it.
Come back tomorrow, or don't.
The cursor will wait.

Your entries live in this browser, on this device.
Not on a server. Nobody else can see them.
History is one tap away. Today is always clean.
Export to keep. Nothing is lost unless you clear your browser data.

---

## the loop, not the ritual

This is not a ritual. It's a loop.
You return because returning is the thing.
The act of showing up is the game mechanic.
There's no streak guilt. No punishment for missing days.
The loop is always open. Come back whenever.

## philosophy

Built for everyone who needs it to work.

No install. No account. No subscription. No internet required.
One file. Runs on old hardware, borrowed computers,
library terminals, USB sticks passed between friends.

Digital simplicity with access in mind is the goal.

If you have every resource available and use this anyway — welcome.
If you're on a borrowed computer with twenty minutes on the clock — welcome.
The utility does not know the difference.

Take what you need, and compost or leave the rest.
Godspeed.
