# Liminal

## What it is

Liminal is a private daily journaling game that asks you one question: *what was one good thing today?*

Once a day, a prompt appears — pulled from a rotating pack of fifteen — and you write into it. That's the whole game. You can save your entry to come back to, export it as a plain text file to keep forever, or let it go entirely. Nothing leaves your device. No account required.

The name fits. It lives at the threshold: between a to-do app and a diary, between holding on and letting go, between the day you had and the one you're stepping into.

---

## How it works

When you open Liminal you get:

- **A daily prompt** — rotates by day of year, never repeats within a cycle. Three prompt packs: *celebration*, *default*, and *tender*, each tuned for different emotional registers
- **One good thing** — a single input line for the thing itself, kept to 140 characters
- **Say more, or don't** — an optional freewrite space beneath it, no pressure
- **Three actions:**
  - `[ save ]` — stores your entry locally in your browser, private to your device
  - `[ export ]` — downloads today's entry as a plain `.txt` file, yours to keep
  - `[ let it go ]` — clears the fields without saving, releases what you wrote
- **My entries** — once you have three or more saves, a history view appears with all past entries, plus bulk export
- **Background image** — drag in a personal photo to sit quietly behind the interface at low opacity; stays until you remove it

The interface is dark, terminal-styled, and deliberately quiet. Buttons only light up while you're typing. Nothing demands your attention.

## Who it's for

Anyone who wants a low-stakes daily practice. Journal writers, bullet journal folks, people who want the benefit of a gratitude log without the app that tracks your streaks and sends you push notifications. People in hard seasons who need a place to name one thing. People in good seasons who want to remember them.

---

## Your data

Liminal uses your browser's local storage. Nothing is sent anywhere. That means:

- Your entries are private to your device and browser
- If you clear your browser data, your entries are gone — export regularly if you want to keep them
- Entries don't sync across devices yet

---

## What it isn't

Not a streak tracker. Not a subscription. Not connected to a server. Not analyzing your entries. Not sending notifications. Not storing anything outside your own browser.

---

## Built with

Vanilla HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. One file. No frameworks, no dependencies, no build step. Runs anywhere you can open a browser.
